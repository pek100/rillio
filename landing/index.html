Open add-ons

Catalogs, subtitles and streams come from third-party add-ons you choose to install, browsable at the community directory. Nothing is bundled, and Rillio itself hosts no content, it is the player and the shelf.

4K HDR and Dolby Vision, in the app

  • Stremio hands these streams to an external player
  • VLC skips their Dolby Vision metadata

    VLC's pipeline does not process the Dolby Vision RPU metadata in these streams, so playback falls back to the base layer. Rillio renders through mpv and libplacebo, whose open-source implementation applies it. Rillio is not Dolby certified and does not claim to be.

  • Rillio plays them natively, right in the app

HDR passthrough on HDR displays, and Dolby Vision metadata processed in-app through mpv and libplacebo, with no fallback to SDR. Exact output depends on your stream, GPU and display.

Your watchlist is not a product

No tracking, no ads, no data business. Here is what that means in practice, and where it stops.

Local-first, no account

Your library and history live on your device by default, not on someone else's server.

Encrypted DNS

Name lookups go out over DNS-over-HTTPS, not in plaintext to whoever is listening.

No open ports

Rillio does not open inbound ports or forward them through your router, so your machine is never left listening for connections.

Downloads stay in their box

Every file path a torrent declares must resolve inside Rillio's own cache folder; a path reaching for anywhere else is rejected and the torrent thrown out. See the threat model for why that matters.

No ads, no tracking

No trackers, no telemetry by default, nothing between you and what you came to watch.

Memory-safe engine

The torrent engine and parsers are Rust, which removes the buffer-overflow class behind real client takeovers. Not immunity, but the most common exploit class is deleted outright.

Auditable

An open-source Rust engine you can read and verify, running locally, not a black box.

Signed updates

Every update is cryptographically signed and verified before it installs.

Where it stops. Some add-ons stream peer-to-peer, and peers on those networks can see your IP address. Rillio does not hide that, and honestly no app can without a VPN. If it matters to you, route Rillio through your own VPN or SOCKS5 proxy.

The threat model, in the open

Media players and torrent engines parse input from strangers by definition. Here is what actually matters today, with an honest status on each, and then the whole species of historical exploits this design is simply immune to.

Executables disguised as media

By far the most common real-world way people get infected from downloads: a fake installer, a "codec pack", or an executable renamed to look like a video, hoping you double-click it.

Covered

Structurally impossible here. Rillio never executes downloaded content; a file is demuxed as media into the player, or it is nothing.

Malicious media hitting the decoder

Crafted video, audio or embedded subtitles that exploit the shared native decode libraries (ffmpeg / libplacebo) every player embeds, ours included. A steady stream of CVEs, industry-wide.

Not yet

This is our honest open gap: decoding is not yet sandboxed away from the app. We track upstream mpv/ffmpeg fixes closely, and process-isolating the decoder is on the roadmap.

Peers can see your IP

On peer-to-peer networks, peers exchange IP addresses by design. No client can hide this on its own, and any that claims to is not being straight with you.

By design · bring a VPN

Privacy-conscious defaults (no inbound port, no UPnP, encrypted DNS), and we say plainly that IP privacy needs your own VPN or SOCKS5 proxy.

Safe against the species

Each of these is a real, documented CVE, patched long ago in its own product. They are here as specimens: Rillio's architecture rules out the entire class, so the species cannot recur here, whatever new specimen appears.

Torrents escaping the download folder

Crafted file names writing outside the download directory, into startup folders and worse. Specimen: libtorrent (2014), still recurring in Deluge (2025).

ImmuneWrite-jailed cache

Parser memory corruption to code execution

A malformed torrent or peer message overflows a hand-written parser and runs attacker code. Specimen: uTorrent CVE-2020-8437.

ImmuneMemory-safe Rust engine

Poisoned subtitle files, auto-fetched

Malicious subtitles pushed up a repository's rankings so the player downloads and parses them with no user action. Specimen: Check Point "Hacked in Translation" (2017), ~200M installs exposed.

ImmuneSSRF-guarded fetch, memory-safe parse

Websites driving the local engine

A web page in your browser calls 127.0.0.1 to control your client or read files through it. Specimen: the Deluge (2025) unauthenticated file read.

ImmuneOrigin allowlist, POST-only, SSRF guard

Update-channel tampering

A hijacked updater turns one trusted app into a malware delivery network in a single push. Specimen: the CCleaner supply-chain attack (2017), ~2.3M machines.

ImmuneSigned, verified updates (minisign)

Every specimen links to its primary source. These bugs are patched in their own products; they are here because they define the classes this software must be built to exclude. No software is invulnerable, including this one, so the code is open for audit.

Questions, answered plainly

Do I need an account?

No. Rillio works fully anonymously from the first launch. Your library, history and settings live in the app on your device. If you ever want them on another machine, export them as a sync code and paste it there.

Where does the content come from?

Rillio ships empty. It is a player and an organizer; catalogs and streams come from third-party add-ons that you choose to install, which you can browse at the community add-on directory. Rillio does not host, index or provide any media, and no add-ons are bundled. What you access through add-ons is your choice and your responsibility, so stick to sources you have the right to use.

Can I bring my Stremio library?

Yes, with one sign-in. Rillio pulls your library and add-ons, stores them locally on your device, and disconnects. Your Stremio account is left exactly as it was.

Is it really free?

Yes. Rillio is free software under the GPL, with no tiers, no ads and no data business. Every line of it is on GitHub for you to read, build and verify.
